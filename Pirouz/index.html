Pirouz (5/1/2022 - 2/28/2023)

Pirouz, an Iranian cheetah, is an endangered species that died on 2/28/2023 due to kidney problems caused by the injection of wrong drugs in childhood and the lack of experience of veterinarians.

Pirouz Species

( Asiatic Cheetah )

The Asiatic cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus venaticus) is a critically endangered cheetah subspecies currently only surviving in Iran. (The Asiatic cheetah became extinct in India in 1948; the cheetahs which were reintroduced in 2022, are the African subspecies, the Southeast African cheetah.) It once occurred from the Arabian Peninsula and the Near East to the Caspian region, Transcaucasus, Kyzylkum Desert and northern South Asia, but was extirpated in these regions during the 20th century. The Asiatic cheetah diverged from the cheetah population in Africa between 32,000 and 67,000 years ago.

Pirouz Death

( 2/28/2023 )

Pirouz was taken to Tehran Veterinary Hospital in the early hours of Friday, february 24 2023 with symptoms of intestinal obstruction. After initial investigations and radiology and ultrasound tests, the veterinarians diagnosed that he had an "acute kidney complication". Pirouz after 10 months of life on february 28 2023, He died due to kidney failure and digestive problems and after peritoneal dialysis and a surgery.
